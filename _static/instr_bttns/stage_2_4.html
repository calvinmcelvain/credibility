Instructions

Stage 2

Your group in Stage 2 is the same five participants as in Stage 1 and your role (Advisor or Investor) is the same as well.

In many ways, Stage 2 is like Stage 1.

  1. Investors will choose whether to invest their endowment in an investment opportunity.
  2. The four Investors in each group will again receive no information about the quality of the investment opportunity. Instead, they will receive a message from the Advisor, either “Invest” or “Keep”. All Investors will see the same message from their group’s Advisor before making their investment decisions.
  3. The conversion rate of ECU to dollars remains $1 per 25 ECU.

Stage 2: Payoffs

There are a number of differences between Stage 1 and Stage 2.

  1. The payoff tables will change for each scenario in Stage 2. Be certain to look at the payoff tables and instructions before making choices in Stage 2, as these will not be the same as Stage 1 or the other scenarios in Stage 2.
  2. In every round of Stage 1, the investors’ initial endowments were always 12 ECU. This is not true in Stage 2. Instead, for each scenario, an endowment will be randomly drawn for each Investor. The next few screens explain exactly how this works and how Investors will choose between “Invest” and “Keep”.

Prizes

For all the scenarios in Stage 2, Investors have a chance of winning a prize worth 400 ECU (400 ECU = $16).

The average amount you will earn as an Investor if you choose Invest is the probability that you will win the prize multiplied by 400.

  • For example, suppose you think there is a 25% chance you will win the prize. The average amount you will earn as an Investor in you choose Invest is .25 x 400 = 100 ECU.
  • If you think there is a 75% chance you will win the prize, the average amount you will earn as an Investor if you choose Invest is .75 x 400 = 300 ECU.
  • The higher the chance you will win the prize, the more you will win on average.

Minimum Endowments

Investors who choose “Keep” will be paid their endowment. In Stage 2, the endowment for each scenario will be randomly drawn between 0 ECU and 400 ECU. All numbers in this range, including 0 and 400, are equally likely. Endowments are drawn separately for each Investor; knowing your endowment does not tell you anything about the endowment for other Investors. Each Investor will choose the minimum endowment for which you will choose Keep.

  • Any endowment below your minimum will result in you choosing to Invest. You will give up your endowment and be paid based on the payoff table for that decision.
  • If an endowment is drawn at or above your minimum, you will choose Keep. You will be paid your endowment.

Choosing a Minimum Endowment

You will use an interface like the one shown on the screen to choose the minimum endowment for which you will select Keep. There is a slider, a text box labeled “minimum endowment” next to the slider, and a text box labeled “probability” below the slider. You can choose a minimum endowment three ways: moving the slider, entering a minimum endowment into the text box labeled “minimum endowment”, or entering a probability of winning the prize into the text box labeled “probability”.

What Minimum Endowment Should You Choose?

Your best choice as an Investor is always to set your minimum endowment exactly equal to the average amount you believe you will earn by choosing Invest. The average value of investing is equal to the probability that you win the prize multiplied by 400.

You do not control the probability that you win the prize. When you change the number in the probability box, what you are entering is your best guess about how likely it is that you will win the prize.

Stage 2 Gameplay

  1. In Stage 2, there will only be two possible quality levels, “Low” and “High”. Each possible quality level is equally likely, like the flip of a coin.
  2. In Stage 2, rather than receiving an estimate, the Advisor will directly observe the true quality level. In other words, the Advisor will be able to send advice based on the actual quality of the investment opportunity rather than a noisy estimate of the quality. As in Stage 1, the Advisor will choose advice for each possible quality level (Low or High).
  3. Unlike Stage 1, Investors will only choose “Invest” or “Keep” if the Advisor advises Invest.
    1. You will keep your endowment for certain if your Advisor advises Keep.
    2. If the Advisor advises Invest, Investors will choose “Invest” or “Keep” based on their choice of a minimum endowment. If the randomly drawn endowment is below their minimum endowment, the Investor chooses Invest. If the randomly drawn endowment is at or above their minimum endowment, the Investor chooses Keep.
  4. Unlike Stage 1, you will not see feedback after choosing in each scenario. You will see your feedback only after completing your decisions in all scenarios.
  5. You will make choices for four scenarios in Stage 2. One of the four scenarios will be randomly chosen to count towards your earnings in today’s experiment.
